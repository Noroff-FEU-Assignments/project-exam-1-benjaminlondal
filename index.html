What is mead?

Mead is an alcoholic beverage, which in it's most base form consists of honey, water and yeast. When the yeast ferments the sugar from the honey it produces alcohol and you get a beverage that can resemble a wine, with honey as the primary contributor to flavour as opposed to grapes. Just like a wine - a mead can be sweet, semi-dry or dry. It can be sparkling or still, high in alcohol or "sessionable" - all depending on the meadmakers intention with the product.
